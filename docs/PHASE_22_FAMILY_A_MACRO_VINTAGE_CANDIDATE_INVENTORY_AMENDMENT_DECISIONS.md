## Exact official-source registry

The artifact freezes exactly these three first-party citations in order. They are metadata
provenance only; generation and verification never browse them.

1. `PHILADELPHIA_FED_RTDSM_OVERVIEW`
   - [Real-Time Data Set for Macroeconomists](https://www.philadelphiafed.org/surveys-and-data/real-time-data-research/real-time-data-set-for-macroeconomists)
   - The Philadelphia Fed describes RTDSM as vintages or snapshots of major macroeconomic series,
     states that complete time-series histories for each vintage are downloadable, permits
     macroeconomic research/verification/policy analysis/forecasting, and updates at month-end.
2. `PHILADELPHIA_FED_RTDSM_PCPI`
   - [Data Files - Real-Time Data Set (PCPI)](https://www.philadelphiafed.org/surveys-and-data/real-time-data-research/pcpi)
   - The PCPI page identifies Consumer Price Index monthly vintages and monthly observations,
     seasonally adjusted, with vintages from `1998:M11` to the present. It states that every vintage
     column contains the entire time-series history available at that vintage.
3. `PHILADELPHIA_FED_ONLINE_TERMS`
   - [Online Terms of Use and Privacy Notice](https://www.philadelphiafed.org/about-us/privacy-notice)
   - The terms permit site content for informational, educational, and research purposes while
     warning that some material may be copyrighted and permission may be required from its owner.

The two documented PCPI spreadsheet surfaces are
`pcpiMvMd.xlsx` (complete monthly-vintage matrix) and
`pcpi_first_second_third.xlsx` (release-value output). Their public availability establishes only a
documented download surface. Phase 22 does not download either file or freeze a URL hash, payload,
schema, observation, or dataset identity.

The official PCPI documentation says monthly vintages reflect data collected electronically shortly
after BLS releases the monthly CPI report and says the annual recalculation of seasonal factors can
revise up to five years of seasonally adjusted observations. It also cautions that CPI ordinarily
has only a first release and latest-revised value rather than meaningful consecutive second and
third releases. These details support a future qualification question; they are not silently
converted into exact availability timestamps.

## Conservative limitations and supporting reconciliation

The Phase 22 candidate remains unqualified for each of these reasons:

- Philadelphia Fed terms state a research-purpose boundary but do not expressly resolve persistent
  database storage, automated model integration, derived outputs, retention after withdrawal,
  third-party material, revocation, or deletion obligations for Fable5's exact use.
- `1998:M11` proves a monthly-vintage history that spans the requested 2000 start, but does not prove
  complete values, lack of gaps, schema stability, or exact usable coverage for every desired date.
- A `yyMmm` vintage label is not an exact UTC release or availability timestamp. The PCPI notes say
  only that monthly vintages reflect data available shortly after the BLS report.
- A future qualification must reconcile each candidate vintage to the official
  [BLS CPI archived release](https://www.bls.gov/bls/news-release/cpi.htm). The archive includes
  2000 releases, exact embargo dates/times in individual releases, and a contemporaneous note about
  the September 28, 2000 revision. This supporting BLS surface is not a second candidate and Phase
  22 performs no reconciliation.
- BLS's [Terms of Service](https://www.bls.gov/developers/termsOfService.htm) state that downloaded
  BLS data have no end-use controls and require retrieval-date citation and a source disclaimer for
  public-API users. Those terms do not enlarge the Philadelphia Fed's rights or complete the future
  combined-source review.
- Candidate metadata does not prove Family A field mapping, point-in-time normalization, quality,
  missingness policy, leakage safety, holdout safety, or research admission.
